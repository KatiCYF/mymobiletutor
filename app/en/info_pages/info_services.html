Services

Turku has a good service network for various essential services provided by both private and public sectors. Get to know the most important services below!

There are different ways to send domestic and international mail. Most common service is Posti, which takes care of the daily mail delivery in Finland. It has locations throughout the city, mostly within other services such as supermarkets. Automated parcel points are quick and easy for parcel deliveries. In addition, you can make the notification of change of address in Posti.

Finland is using Euro as a currency. You can withdraw money from cash-machines known as OTTO (pankkiautomaatti) with international credit cards or Finnish debit cards. They work 24/7 and the withdrawal fee depends on your card. Withdrawal is free of charge with Finnish debit cards.

Almost all the service providers, amongst the smallest retailers, accept credit cards (usually Visa and Master Card). It is wise to ask in advance if they accept your card. Mobile payment is not common yet in shops and restaurants.

Paying bills in Finland is usually done via e-banking. Payments made at the bank by a bank officer usually cost around 6-8 euros. You cannot use cheques to pay your bills.

There are several banks in Finland and in Turku area. If you are staying in Finland only for a short period, you don’t have to open a bank account here as it requires various documents.

Finland has one of the lowest mobile communication prices in Europe in addition to high-quality networks. Package offers may have great variations, so it is advisable to check few service providers before making a decision. Data packages usually cost around 20-30 euros/month.

Foreigners cannot have subscriptions unless they have lived in Finland for 2 years. The best alternative is to get pre-paid connection and they don’t require deposits or official documents. Pre-paid connections can be bought in any R-Kioski (small retailer around the city) and in the operator shops.

Employment office (TE-palvelut) is mainly for graduates when they are looking for work. You can find some general information about working and living in Finland from their pages. Remember when you graduate and you are still staying in Finland, you need to register to employment office as a jobseeker to be able to receive unemployment security compensation.

Finnish Immigration Service (Maahanmuuttovirasto) gives information on resident permits and other issues concerning immigrants in Finland.

KELA provides national health insurance and social benefits. It provides beneficial information when moving to Finland.

Local Register Office (Maistraatti) takes care of various registering services, both online and offline. Every foreigner residing in Finland has to register themselves to Local registry office. After registering you will have a Finnish ID number, which will help you to open a bank account, make phone contracts etc.
