Health

Healthcare has high quality in Finland. All permanent residents in Finland can use public healthcare with low price. EU, Iceland, Norway, Switzerland and Liechtenstein citizens can use public services with European Health Insurance Card.

People coming from other countries can use private healthcare by paying the service fees or using travel insurance. Private health care is expensive without insurance. In emergency situation you will be taken care of in the hospital, but the costs can be collected from you afterwards.

Students can use student healthcare services, if you have paid the fee while registration (See Student healthcare below for more information).

In life-threatening situations use an emergency number 112. There is also a free app available called ‘112 Suomi’, which helps to locate you faster and accurately when in need. In addition, it will send information aout danger, if you allow notifications.

In urgent situations you call to hospital’s own emergency number for further guidance. In Turku area, the number is 02 313 8800.

There are two types of public healthcare: primary healthcare is provided by municipal health centers (terveysasemat), and specialized medical care provided by district hospitals (sairaalat).

If you are eligible to use public healthcare, after registering your address you will be automatically registered to a health center close to you. You can only make appointments to that specific health center. If it is not urgent illness, the waiting period can be long for public centers, even months. Urgent illnesses and accidents are of course handled quickly.

Health care in Turku

You can use your travel/healthcare insurance or pay the private healthcare services by yourself. The services cover almost everything from basic care to surgeries and it is fast to get in. For basic care the fee is around 60-80 euros/20 min. In Turku area there are many service providers for both health and dental care (e.g. Mehiläinen and Terveystalo).

In Autumn period 2020, you can use FSHS (Finnish Student Health Service, YTHS in Finnish) if you are studying at degree program or having an exchange period at the University of Turku and have paid student union membership fee.

NOTE! Starting in January 2021, students who are entitled to use the services (only degree students) of FSHS will pay a healthcare fee to Kela. No bill will be sent on the healthcare fee, and thus the student has to pay the fee to Kela on his or her own initiative. More information about the new fee.

FSHS provides general, mental and oral healthcare. Most of the services are free of charge, only special care has small fees.

NOTE! Remember to cancel your appointment! The penalty fee for no-show is €35.

Medication (both prescribed and non-prescribed) in Finland is only available in pharmacies (Apteekki). Pharmacies are usually open during normal business hours. In Turku city center, Yliopiston Apteekki (Yliopistonkatu 25) is open daily 07.00-23.00 for urgent needs.
