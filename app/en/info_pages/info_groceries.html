There are three main retailers (S-Group, K-Group and Lidl) with various different stores and few smaller service providers. Nowadays stores are free to choose their own opening hours and many stores are open daily from early morning until late evening. Hyper and supermarkets have cheaper prices than small food stores, yet smaller stores are usually more conveniently located within neighbourhoods.

In Turku, we finally have a grocery store open 24/7. Check out where!

In Finland, selling alcohol is highly regulated. Only mild liquors are sold in grocery stores from 9 am to 9 pm and other liquors in a chain called Alko. The age limit for beer and wines is 18 years and hard liquors 20 years.

There are various specialty food stores in Turku to cater your special needs.

Market Hall (Kauppahalli) is located in the city center and provides a special place for grocery shopping. It includes various bakeries, butchers, fishmongers, cheese shops, tea, coffee and spice retailers, restaurants and cafes.

Turku Market Square (Turun kauppatori) is under renovation at the moment but you can still find stalls full of fresh fruits, vegetables, berries, fish, flowers and handicrafts. In Christmas time the Market Square becomes a winter wonderland.

Vegekauppa (webpages only in Finnish) provides vegetarian food options and is located in Forum in the city center.

Ruohonjuuri (webpages only in Finnish) provides organic food, dietary supplements and natural cosmetics and is located in Eerikinkatu 17.

In addition there are various oriental stores in the city center providing especially Chinese, Thai and Arabic specialties. There is also a halal meat store in Eerikinkatu 5, which sells fresh and affordable meat.
