Shopping

There are various shopping possibilities in Turku. In addition to couple of malls and department stores, there are more and more unique boutiques and second hand stores throughtout the city. You may also find interesting pop-up stores and fairs all year round.

Hansakortteli is a mall located in the main square of Turku where you can find a large variety of clothing and other stores including sports and electronics housing a total over 150 businesses. The mall also has a generous food court.

Forum Kortteli is a shopping center located in the heart of Turku, right across the street from the Kauppatori marketplace. There are many small service providers and retailers and there are often interesting pop-up stores from Finnish designers.

KOP-kolmio is a small shopping center located in the heart of city, next to Market Hall (Kauppahalli). (Internet pages only in Finnish.)

Skanssi is a mall 5 km from Turku housing a total of 90 businesses ranging from clothing to cafés and grocery stores.

Mylly is another big mall about 9 km from Turku hosting more than 150 businesses from grocieries and fashion to different services (e.g. hairdresser and pharmacies) and restaurants.

In the west (about 4 km from the center) there is shopping area called Länsikeskus from where you can find hypermarkets (e.g. Prisma, Citymarket) and many other stores, second hand shops and some restaurants (Länsikeskus on the map).

Stockmann (webpages only in Finnish) is a large department store located in the Hansa shopping center. Stockmann offers a large range of products from cosmetics and fashion to interior decoration and a well-stocked grocery store.

Sokos Wiklund (webpages only in Finnish) is a large department store across the main square from Hansa. Wiklund offers a large range of cosmetics and fashion goods.

Ikea (webpages only in Finnish) is a good place to find affordable furniture and design elements. There is an Ikea store located near Turku in Raisio a bus trip away. The following bus connections arrive nearby: 2, 302, 303, 220, 221, 300. Check the correct buses and routes from Föli.

Clas Ohlson (webpages only in Finnish) is a hardware store with location in the center of Turku. It is convenient for your daily hardware needs.

Clothes and household goods

Unfortunately almost all webpages are only in Finnish.

KirppisCenter offers two biggest second hand malls (Manhattan and Länsikeskus) in Turku, both few kilometers from the city center. These venues offer a possibility to bring your own clothes and other goods for sale or find something for yourself.

Laiskan kirppis is located at Vanha Hämeentie 29 and has mostly clothes and other smaller goods. It has the lowest price in Turku to sell your own extra goods.

Sifonki is a second hand store specialized in more expensive women's clothes, shoes and accessories just in the city center in Kauppiaskatu 10.

Pelastusarmeija (Salvation Army) offers few different locations for their flee markets in Turku. By buying from them, you support people in need. You can donate goods for sale or find something for yourself.

Puutorin kirppis is a modern second hand store in Brahenkatu 14. You can find mostly clothes and small household goods or you can bring your own goods for sale.

Maanantaimarket (Monday market) is also located in the city center at Kristiinankatu 6. You can find mostly clothes and small household goods or you can bring your own goods for sale.

UFF Turku has extensive collection of second hand clothes and is situated in the city center in Humalistonkatu 5.

Furniture and other larger goods

Ekotori is a Turku-based non-profit organization whose mission is to create pro-environmental awareness and facilitate recycling. They have two locations within the city (Rautakatu 12 and Rieskalähteentie 74), where you can find furniture, bicycles, electronics etc. You can donate your extra goods for them to re-sell.

Kontti sells many kinds of goods and it has good selection of furniture. They also pick-up furniture for sale or deliver the ones you’ve bought. It is located in Viilarinkatu 5. They use majority of their profit for charity.

Tori.fi is a huge digital market place for second hand goods and a great place to sell and find necessities from clothes to bicycles and from electronics to rental houses.

In addition there are various pop-up events for second hand goods such as Kårenin kirppis, Monthly flea market at Book Café and Siivouspäivä (Cleaning Day), which is a new day of celebration for friends of flea markets and recycling. Anyone can offer their second hand items up for sale on the streets, yards and at home, as well as make the best finds few times a year.
