Safety

Finland is a safe country due to its stable society and efficient police force. According to the Global Peace Index 2016, Finland is the world’s 11th safest country. Petty theft can occur, and you should be careful in the evening during weekends because drunken people can be aggressive.

In case of any emergency call 112. We recommend you download the app called ‘112 Suomi’. Any emergency calls made through the app helps to locate you immediately.

The police force in Finland is well educated and reliable. In case of an emergency call 112. Finland is a highly regulated country, and you need permission for things such as public events and demonstrations. Apply for a permit from the police either online or at the police station.

In Finland, violence is mostly domestic, or at the very least the offender and victim know one another. Random acts of serious violence are unusual. Most common crimes in Turku are bicycle thefts, even after carefully securing your bike.

The easiest way to report minor crimes is by using the online reporting system with your banking codes or mobile verification. In case of a serious crime or an accident, always call 112.

Traffic safety is quite high in Finland because people usually follow traffic regulations closely. Wearing a seatbelt is mandatory in cars and in long distance buses. Helmets are compulsory for motorcyclists and highly recommended for cyclists as well.

Autumn and winter can be very dark in Finland. Therefore, it is recommended to use a reflector to be visible in traffic. When using a bike, it is compulsory to use a lamp when it is dark.

In Finland, 10 % of the population has an immigrant background, and the majority live in cities. Unfortunately, racism and hate speech have increased during the past few years due to the economic downturn and increased unemployment.

Fortunately, Turku is quite a multicultural city because of a high number of students and other immigrants, so people accept foreigners quite well. Usually, immigrant women face less opposition than men.

Finns are known for good English skills, but some are afraid to speak the language. Please ask if they can speak English and they usually do a little, at least.

Finland is a safe country with a lack of seismic activity and the rarity of big storms. Winter (November-February) can be cold, even -30 Celsius, so remember to wear warm clothes! Flooding can be a problem in spring because of melting snow.

Electricity supply and transportation can suffer from severe weather, and delays can occur especially during heavy snow.
